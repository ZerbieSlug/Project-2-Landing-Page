How to make Mexican Red Rice

Simple, Quick, Delicious Steps

While rice is not exclusive to Mexico, it is a versitile grain with a lot of potential. They're simple to make, requires few ingredients, and takes less than half an hour to prepare.
With rice, you can...
- Use it as a side dish to complement your meals
- Make burritos more fulfilling
- Share around with the family for a few days
- ...And more!
In this recipe I'll show you how to make the iconic Mexican Red Rice, what ingredients you'll need, and the steps you'll have to take to create this amazing side and filler dish!

Preparations (Ingredients & Utensils)

Ingredients You'll Need:

  1. Corn Oil (or Olive Oil)
  2. 1 Cup of Rice
  3. 1 Tomato (2 for added Surface jam)
  4. A Slice of Onion
  5. 2 'Teeth' of Garlic
  6. 1 hefty tbsp of Chicken Bouillon
  7. 1.5-2 cups of water

Utensils You'll Need:

  1. A deep pan for rice
  2. A Blender
  3. A spatula for stirring
  4. A knife to cut ingredients
Instructions (How to cook)
  1. On low heat, pour some corn oil onto the pan, making sure to spread it around to cover the bottom.
  2. Cut a ring-slice off a whole Onion, dice it up, and put them onto the pan. Occasionally stir the onions.
  3. When the onions start to show signs of yellowing/browning, pour in your cup of rice, stirring occasionally. Raise your heat to medium at this point.
  4. With a blender in reach, grab a tomato and two ‘teeth’ of garlic. Pouring 1.5 cups of water onto the blender, proceed to blend until it becomes a liquid.
  5. With enough stirring, once the rice start to lightly brown, pour in your tomato blend and stir briefly.
  6. Get a spoonful of Chicken Bouillon and mix it with the soupy mixture.
  7. Once the “soup” bubbles, lower the heat and put a lid on the pan. In around 15-20 minutes, the water will evaporate, leaving behind puffy and red rice.
    • Check in every now and then at this point, make a little hole into the rice to see the bottom. If there remains a fairly thin yolk-like layer of water at the bottom, turn off the heat.

(Tips)

With those nice and toasty onions mixed with the salty and delicious tomato bouillon combo,
you and your family will enjoy this iconic side-dish!
